Our Story

Born in the Smoke
of Old Lahore

"My grandfather lit the first fire in a corner of Anarkali Bazaar with nothing but an iron wok and a family recipe. That fire has never gone out."

In 1987, Ustad Bashir Ahmad set up a single karahi stall on the streets of Gulberg. Word spread — not through advertising, but through the irresistible pull of smoke rising above the rooftops. Thirty-seven years later, Lahori Flames is a Lahore institution, carrying the same hand-ground masalas, the same slow-fire philosophy, and the same obsessive dedication to flavour that made people queue around the block in the eighties. Nothing has changed. That's the point.
